Now we’d like to provide you with more information about what has been happening while you’ve been waiting.

You and the estimator were both eligible to receive additional bonuses for participating. There was {{ Constants.appeal_reward }} to be split evenly between you and the estimator, for {{ Constants.appeal_reward_split }} apiece. The estimator had the option to pay {{ Constants.appeal_cost }} to send his/her case to a third-party judge, another participant. If the estimator chose not to send the case to the judge, the bonus would continue to be split evenly between you and the estimator, for {{ Constants.appeal_reward_split }} apiece.

If the estimator chose to pay {{ Constants.appeal_cost }} to send the case to the judge, the judge would decide whether you treated the estimator fairly in the dots-estimation task. If the judge decided that you treated the estimator unfairly, the estimator would be awarded the entire {{ Constants.appeal_reward }} bonus (and you would receive no bonus). If the judge decided that you treated the estimator fairly, the bonus would continue to be split evenly for {{ Constants.appeal_reward_split }} apiece.
